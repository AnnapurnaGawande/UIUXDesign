Improving the Fashion E-commerce Checkout UX

A UX case study focused on simplifying and optimizing the checkout experience for an online fashion store.

Project Overview

This project aimed to address high cart abandonment rates caused by a complicated checkout process. The redesigned experience emphasized speed, simplicity, and trust.

  • Platform: Web & Mobile Web
  • Role: UX Designer
  • Tools: Figma, Miro, Adobe XD
  • Timeline: 4 Weeks

Problem & User Pain Points

The original checkout flow had too many steps, unclear progress indicators, and lacked mobile responsiveness. Users felt frustrated with repeated form inputs, hidden fees, and forced account creation.

User Research & Personas

We interviewed 12 online shoppers and analyzed heatmaps from previous sessions. Key insights led to two personas:

  • Priya (24, Student): Mobile shopper, values speed and simplicity.
  • Rahul (35, Working Professional): Prioritizes security and dislikes surprises during checkout.

User Flow

The revised flow was streamlined to just three clear stages: Cart → Delivery Info → Payment & Review. Progress bars and back buttons helped users feel in control.

UX Fixes You Made

  • Simplified the form layout and introduced autofill support.
  • Enabled guest checkout with email confirmation.
  • Added trust badges and delivery estimate previews.
  • Reduced the total steps from 5 to 3 without loss of detail.

Key Takeaways

By focusing on real pain points and reducing friction, the new checkout experience led to a 20% improvement in completion rates and higher customer satisfaction scores. Clear communication, mobile-first design, and user trust were the keys to success.

Case Study Summary

This project highlights how thoughtful UX design can directly improve business metrics and customer retention. The optimized checkout is now being tested in A/B deployments across multiple regions.
