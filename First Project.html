Case Study: Improving the Fashion E-commerce Checkout UX

Problem & User Pain Points

Users often abandon their carts during the checkout process due to a long, confusing flow, unexpected costs, and a lack of trust in payment security. The existing checkout was cluttered, non-mobile-friendly, and required too many steps to complete.

User Research & Personas

Interviews with 12 fashion shoppers (ages 20–35) revealed a desire for speed, simplicity, and clear progress indicators. Two key personas emerged:

  • Alicia (26, Student): Shops frequently via mobile, values quick, secure checkouts.
  • David (32, Designer): Wants transparency and dislikes creating accounts just to purchase.

User Flow

The redesigned flow: Cart → Shipping Info → Payment → Review & Confirm. The revised process reduced steps and clarified each action.

UX Fixes You Made

  • Condensed checkout into 3 simple, clear steps.
  • Introduced guest checkout to reduce friction.
  • Improved mobile responsiveness and readability.
  • Added trust signals (badges, reviews) at payment.
  • Implemented autofill and address lookup for speed.

Key Takeaways

Simplifying the checkout flow led to a 22% increase in completed purchases during testing. The project reinforced the value of user feedback, fast iteration, and designing with real pain points in mind.
